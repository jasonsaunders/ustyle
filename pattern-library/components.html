Breadcrumbs Github: _breadcrumbs.scss

Breadcrumbs are used to provide signposting of a users location and to enable quick navigation around the site. They are only to be used as a secondary means of navigation however. Pages should also be navigable through other means.

Example

  • uSwitch.com
  • Credit report
  • Credit reports guides
  • Statutory credit reports

               <ul class="us-crumbs">
                 <li class="us-crumbs__item"><a href="/" class="us-crumbs__link">uSwitch.com</a></li>
                 <li class="us-crumbs__item"><a class="us-crumbs__link" href="http://www.uswitch.com/credit-reports/">Credit report</a></li>
                 <li class="us-crumbs__item"><a class="us-crumbs__link" href="http://www.uswitch.com/credit-reports/guides/">Credit reports guides</a></li>
                 <li class="us-crumbs__item">Statutory credit reports</li>
               </ul>

Buttons Github: _button.scss

Our buttons used across the site. The states give different use cases. Both <a> and <button> tags are supported. Please use role='button' when using in a <a> tag.

States

  • .us-btn--primary - Used a save/update button
  • .us-btn--action - Navigational button
  • .us-btn--secondary - Secondary
  • .us-btn--hero - Navy outline used for buttons on hero banners
  • .us-btn--reversed - White outline for dark backgrounds
  • .us-btn--large - Larger button for heros
  • .us-btn--small - Smaller button for mobile tables
  • .us-btn--blocked - Full width button
  • .us-btn--link - link style button
  • .us-btn--stronger - Emphasis button
  • .us-btn--disabled - Disabled styling (can also be styled with :disabled)

Content group Github: _content-group.scss

The .us-content-group is a separator element for content groupings. Ideal to split up landing pages with reasonable padding.

States

  • .mobile-bordered - Adds a smaller border and padding to mobile devices.

USPs Github: _usp.scss

USPs (Unique Selling Points) are used within tables and on splash pages to highlight offers within a deal, and also to differentiate deals.

General guidelines; There should only be one USP per deal. Text within USPs should be written in sentence case (not uppercase).

States

  • .us-usp--blue - uSwitch related
  • .us-usp--orange - Rewards (money/points)
  • .us-usp--purple - Gifts eg. free TV
  • .us-usp--yellow - Vouchers
  • .us-usp--typecyan - Micro persuasions eg. most popular, half price
  • .us-usp--green - Renewable and environmentally friendly
  • .us-usp--navy - Generic vertical specific USP
  • .us-usp--cyan - Generic vertical specific USP
  • .us-usp--typegrey - Generic vertical specific USP
  • .us-usp--red - Generic vertical specific USP
