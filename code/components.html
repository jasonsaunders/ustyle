Breadcrumbs Github: _breadcrumbs.scss

Breadcrumbs are used to provide signposting of a users location and to enable quick navigation around the site. They are only to be used as a secondary means of navigation however. Pages should also be navigable through other means.

Example

  • uSwitch.com
  • Credit report
  • Credit reports guides
  • Statutory credit reports

               <ul class="us-crumbs">
                 <li class="us-crumbs__item"><a href="/" class="us-crumbs__link">uSwitch.com</a></li>
                 <li class="us-crumbs__item"><a class="us-crumbs__link" href="http://www.uswitch.com/credit-reports/">Credit report</a></li>
                 <li class="us-crumbs__item"><a class="us-crumbs__link" href="http://www.uswitch.com/credit-reports/guides/">Credit reports guides</a></li>
                 <li class="us-crumbs__item">Statutory credit reports</li>
               </ul>

Buttons Github: _button.scss

The default .us-btn button. There is a larger version of the button as well. Each of these styles adheres to the uSwitch branding colours. Currently we support anchor tags and button tags as the main ussage

  • .us-btn--primary - Used a save/update button
  • .us-btn--action - Navigational button
  • .us-btn--secondary - Secondary
  • .us-btn--hero - Navy outline used for buttons on hero banners
  • .us-btn--reversed - White outline for dark backgrounds
  • .us-btn--arrowed , .us-btn--arrowed-right - Arrowed button used for CTAs (right pointing)
  • .us-btn--arrowed-left - Left pointing variation of arrowed buttons
  • .us-btn--large - Larger button for heros
  • .us-btn--small - Smaller button for mobile tables
  • .us-btn--blocked - Full width button
  • .us-btn--link - link style button
  • .disabled - Disabled styling (can also be styled with :disabled)

Content group Github: _content-group.scss

The .us-content-group is a separator element for content groupings. Ideal to split up landing pages with reasonable padding

  • .mobile-bordered - Adds a smaller border and padding to mobile devices. By default there is none

CTAs Github: _cta.scss

Used within guide and news articles, these CTAs are intended to not interupt the content but to integrate in more subtle means.

Example

It’s your report

Join the campaign to fight for free annual credit reports for everyone

Sign the petition 

               <section class="us-cta cta cta-column">
                  <form>
                    <h3 class="us-cta__title">It’s your report</h3>
                    <p>Join the campaign to fight for free annual credit reports for everyone</p>
                     <a href="/money/itsmyreport/" class="us-btn us-btn--arrowed us-btn--primary arrowed js-track-event" data-track-category="money-insurance-guides-cta" data-track-action="click - large quote box" data-track-label="/money/itsmyreport/" rel="nofollow">Sign the petition</a>
                  </form>

News article Github: _news-article.scss

The news item article standard. Built with .us-news-item. The news item image is a background image as the problem of not displaying <img> tags on mobile hasn't been resolved.

Example

Consumer rights 14 Nov 2014

Customer uses promoted tweet to complain about BA

Sign of things to come? Social media helps to empower disgruntled consumer amid lost luggage row

More consumer rights 

               <div class='us-article-group'>

                   <a class='us-news-item-link' href='#'>
                     <div class='us-news-item-image-container'>

                       <span class='date'>14 Nov 2014</span>

Tabs Github: _tabs.scss

These are the basic tabs used for landing pages. You can either reference a data-target for the urls or href for the target tabs. They have a non-js fallback and fold over to accordion when in small tablet view. They are initialised on page load if the tabs classes exist. To initialise call var tabs = new Tabs()
